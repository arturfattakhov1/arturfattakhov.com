# Content roadmap

This roadmap protects factual accuracy while the personal knowledge website grows. Publish only information supported by a primary source, an official profile, or records approved by Artur Fattakhov. Keep Russian and English pages aligned, but use natural translations rather than mechanically identical wording.

## About improvements

- Review the approved biography for clarity and add dates only when verified.
- Add education and professional-history entries from source documents.
- Link every factual claim that benefits from evidence to a stable primary source.
- Keep the About page as the principal `ProfilePage` for the Person entity and maintain its direct link to the official Profiles hub.

## Research improvements

- Define research questions and areas of interest without implying completed outcomes.
- Add current research entries only after scope, status, and wording are approved.
- Connect research entries to verified publications, identifiers, datasets, or project pages when available.
- Distinguish interests, work in progress, and completed research clearly.

## Publications plan

- Collect authoritative bibliographic records before creating entries.
- Store title, complete author list, publication date, venue, abstract, language, DOI, source URL, and identifier links when available.
- Create one canonical page per publication and add `ScholarlyArticle` only when required fields are verified.
- Reconcile each entry with ORCID, Google Scholar, ResearchGate, and Web of Science without copying unverified citation counts.

## Projects plan

- Publish a project only after its public name, scope, status, role, and URL are approved.
- Separate active work, archived work, and exploratory directions.
- Describe the personal role precisely and avoid unsupported outcome claims.
- Link project pages to relevant research and publications only where the relationship is documented.

## Media plan

- Create records only for confirmed interviews, articles, videos, podcasts, or public appearances.
- Store publisher, title, format, publication date, original URL, language, and a short factual summary.
- Prefer original publisher URLs and preserve an archive reference when appropriate.
- Add `VideoObject`, `PodcastEpisode`, or `Article` schema only when the source data supports it.

## Blog plan

- Establish editorial categories around veterinary medicine, clinical reasoning, research practice, and responsible technology use.
- Require an author, publication date, update date, summary, canonical URL, and reviewed references for each article.
- Add medical-context disclaimers where needed and avoid individualized veterinary advice.
- Publish fewer durable articles rather than thin pages created only for keywords.
- Interlink articles with relevant Research, Publications, Projects, and About sections.

## 90-day SEO plan

### Days 1–30: indexing baseline

- Register the domain in Google Search Console, Yandex Webmaster, and Bing Webmaster Tools.
- Submit the sitemap index and verify that both language versions are discovered.
- Record baseline indexing coverage, branded queries, crawl issues, and page experience data.
- Validate the Person, WebSite, and ProfilePage graph after deployment.
- Prepare a fact-review checklist and a source record for every new entity claim.

### Days 31–60: authoritative content

- Approve and publish the first verified About, research, or CV additions.
- Add publication records only after bibliographic reconciliation.
- Publish one or two substantial bilingual knowledge articles with primary references.
- Strengthen contextual internal links between new content and the entity pages.
- Review search-console queries for language and intent gaps without changing factual positioning.

### Days 61–90: consolidation

- Review index coverage, duplicate canonical reports, hreflang signals, and sitemap processing.
- Improve pages with impressions but unclear search snippets using accurate titles and descriptions.
- Add verified media or project records if source material is available.
- Check external profile consistency for name, canonical website URL, and reciprocal links where each platform permits them.
- Set a quarterly cadence for technical audits, identity-data review, link checking, and content updates.

## Human decisions required before publication

- Which education, employment, and clinical-practice records are approved for public display.
- Which research activities may be described publicly and how their status should be labelled.
- Which publication records are authoritative when profiles disagree.
- Which projects and media records may use public names.
- Editorial review responsibility for veterinary and research content.
